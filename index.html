About

I am a visual journalist at The Economist, where I make graphics and interactives. I am also one of the co-founders of Politibot, a chatbot about Spanish and European politics. Previously, I worked in Madrid at Populate, a civic tech studio.

I really enjoy the little of visualisation design.

UK General election 2019

The Economist

December 5, 2019

During the election night we ran a live forecast that predicted the seat count well in advance of the final result. The main map lets you slice the constituencies by Brexit vote, age, race, density and income. I designed the pages and coded the interactive graphics. With G. Elliot Morris, Evan Hensleigh and James Fransham.

Democratic primaries 2020

The Economist

August 20, 2019

The 2020 Democratic primary race is the most crowded in decades. This poll tracker uses innovative statistical methods to asses the state of the primary. I designed and developed the page and the interactive graphics. G. Elliot Morris wrote the statistical model and Evan Hensleigh assisted with the backend.

The Big Mac index

The Economist

July 11, 2018

The Big Mac index is the classic guide for currency comparison. It has been running on the paper since the 80s and as an interactive since 2012. Our redesign made it responsive and more explanatory. Read more on Source.

Who’s ahead in the mid-term race?

The Economist

May 24, 2018

Forecast model of the 2016 mid-term election. After weeks of discussion we ended up with a design that prioritises straight chances —2 in 3 rather than 70%— and emphasises ranges. With Dan Rosenheck, Matt McLean and Evan Hensleigh.
